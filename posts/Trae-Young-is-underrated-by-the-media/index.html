Trae Young is underrated by the media

nba
basketball
media
Author

Anthony Rubek

Published

November 5, 2024

Atlanta Hawks point guard Trae Young is an established star in the NBA. This past season, he averaged 25.7 points per game and a career-high 10.8 assists per game. His play also earned in a spot in the NBA All Star Game.

However, when big media outlets yearly top 100 NBA player lists came out this past summer, Young found himself placed lower than his stats suggest. ESPN had Young at No. 37 on its list while CBS had him at No. 35. Both lists had him as the 12th ranked point guard behind the following: Dallas’ Luka Doncic, Golden State’s Stephen Curry, New York’s Jalen Brunson, Milwaukee’s Damian Lillard, Indiana’s Tyrese Haliburton, Memphis’ Ja Morant, Philadelphia’s Tyrese Maxey, Denver’s Jamal Murray, Sacramento’s De’Aaron Fox, Dallas’ Kyrie Irving, and Boston’s Jrue Holiday.

Looking over key statistics from the 2023-24 season as well as the early results of the 2024-25 campaign, it is easy to tell that Young is greatly underrated by the media.

Young’s most known trait has been his passing. Ever since he entered the league in 2018, Young has been near the top of the league in assists. This didn’t change last season, despite him missing time.

Code 
NBA23total <- read_csv("NBAtotals2324.csv")

TraeYoung <- NBA23total |>
  filter(Player == "Trae Young")

ggplot() + 
  geom_point(data = NBA23total, aes(x = G, y = AST), color = "black") + 
  geom_point(data = TraeYoung, aes(x = G, y = AST), color = "#E03A3E") + 
  geom_text_repel(
    data = TraeYoung,
    aes(x = G, y = AST, label = Player),
    color = "black"
   ) + theme_minimal() +
  labs(y = "Total Assists",
       x = "Games Played",
       title = "Trae Young passes the ball like no other",
       subtitle = "Despite playing in only 54 games, Young still finished in the top 10 in total assists",
       caption="Source: Basketball Reference | By Anthony Rubek") +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 10),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8),
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
  )

Young’s 583 assists put him at eighth in the league. He would have finished even higher if he had played all 82 games.

What about defense? By far the biggest knock on the three-time all star’s game has been his defending. However, Young has improved a lot over time with his steals per game showing that.

Code 
nba2324 <- read_csv("NBApergame2324.csv")

nba2324G <- nba2324 |> 
  filter(
    G >= 50
  )

Trae <- nba2324G |>
  filter(Player == "Trae Young")

ggplot() +
  geom_beeswarm(
    data = nba2324G,
    aes(x = Pos, y = STL),
    color = "grey"
  ) +
  geom_beeswarm(
    data = Trae,
    aes(x = Pos, y = STL),
    color = "#E03A3E"
  ) + geom_text_repel(

The top 100 players lists came out before the season started, serving as a prediction of who will be the best this coming year. Three weeks in, we can see how Young fares compared to the 12 point guards ranked above him.

Young has exceeded projections so far, leading all point guards in assists per game while coming in at sixth in points per game. It is clear that Young should not have been ranked around the range of guys like Jrue Holiday (No. 36 ESPN, No. 32 CBS) and Jamal Murray (No. 31 ESPN, No. 34 CBS). Instead, Young should have been in discussions with the top of the list like Luka Dončić (No. 2 ESPN, No. 3 CBS).
